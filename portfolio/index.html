A large scale retailer with global presence wanted to identify professional (surrogate) shoppers who take benefit from subsidised prices and shop in bulk to further sell at premium rates, hurting the company's business.


CRM data was available, and an anomaly detection model was trained to identify shoppers buying products with the goal of profit-making. The problem was challenging because the stores had enforced rule based constraints on some of these products, and there were products whose purchase quantity or price, when compared to other products did not trigger rules. Explainable surrogate models were also trained in order to make the decision-making rationale of the AI quite clear to business.


Within 2 months, 3k+ such shoppers were identified in collaboration with the store management in different regions.
